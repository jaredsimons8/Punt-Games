What Is a Punt Game?

A Punt Game is a regular season MLB game in which a manager makes a deliberate, pre-game decision to field a strategically weakened lineup — typically resting 3 or more everyday starters — in a game the team has implicitly conceded before the first pitch. A punt game is still a punt game even if the team wins.

📋 The Definition

The decision is intentional and calculated, not a product of injury or circumstance, and is almost always made in the context of a series already clinched or a favorable upcoming schedule. A team throwing their ace cannot be punting — the starting pitcher is the clearest indicator of intent. Punt games occur almost exclusively after Memorial Day, when standings are meaningful and the cost of a loss is real.

⚙️ How It's Tracked

Every game is scored 0–10 using FIP-based pitching matchups, weighted lineup absences (adjusted for injuries and known platoons), series context, and team quality. One hard rule: if a team is starting their ace, the game cannot be a punt — full stop. April and May are a calibration window, building baselines before detection activates June 1.

⚾ The Story Behind It

This started as a personal project from a lifelong Yankees fan who noticed something nobody was officially tracking — teams quietly giving away games that matter in the standings. The idea grew into a full analytics system covering all 30 teams equally. It's a fan-built, original stat with no official MLB equivalent. 🚫 Ace Disqualifier — Automatic Zero
If a team is starting their ace, the punt score is immediately set to 0 regardless of any other signals. Two conditions trigger this: the SP's FIP ≤ 3.50 (league-wide ace threshold), or the SP's FIP is within 0.40 of that team's own season-best (their relative ace). You don't punt when you're throwing your best arm.
